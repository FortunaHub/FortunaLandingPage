# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Platform engineering, Kubernetes security, SecOps, and DevSecOps leaders evaluating a control plane for cluster risk, workload exposure, Runtime Network visibility, attack paths, SBOM/CVE evidence, identity and RBAC context, and runtime signals. They arrive with a high technical baseline and need to understand whether FortunaHub can replace fragmented security views with one credible workflow.

## Product Purpose

FortunaHub presents Fortuna as a Kubernetes risk operations platform: SBOM and OSV-backed CVE evidence, Findings Queue triage with one unified risk score, Attack Paths, Runtime Network, Kubernetes Inventory and identity context, Policy Rules, Pipeline & Runtime Health, Reports, and multi-cluster telemetry. The landing page should make the product feel concrete, inspectable, and trustworthy enough to request a demo.

## Brand Personality

Precise, defensive, operational. The tone should feel technically fluent and confident without falling into generic cybersecurity drama or vague SaaS claims.

## Anti-references

Avoid template cybersecurity visuals, generic glowing network diagrams, abstract security buzzwords, and repetitive card grids that could belong to any cloud security vendor. Do not hide the product behind decorative effects when screenshots or workflow evidence are available.

## Design Principles

- Show the control plane early: use real product imagery and workflow evidence before abstract brand decoration.
- Make risk understandable: organize technical capabilities around the decisions teams need to make.
- Earn trust through specificity: prefer concrete signals, data types, and workflow names over broad claims.
- Keep urgency controlled: security tone should be serious and sharp, not alarmist.
- Design for technical scanning: let evaluators quickly locate capabilities, integrations, and proof.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard navigation, focus visibility, and reduced-motion alternatives. The design should remain legible for users scanning dense technical content in dark-mode environments.
